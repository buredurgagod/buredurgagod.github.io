My Workflow

My Workflow

Each project is unique, so I follow an adaptive and iterative design process that suits the projects requirement. Each process varies depending upon the services required which includes web/mobile app design and user experience design.

Having workflow or a design process provides me with a framework on how to best proceed when faced with a problem breaking it down into a series of interconnected systems. It is difficult to just highlight all the details in a single mockup, for example it can consist of many states, hidden elements, user cases and scenarios, transitions and visual hints. To understand all of this, going through a design process provides me with a holistic overview.

My workflow can be broken down into 7 stages. The stages are ordered so that it includes a feedback-and-approval method before proceeding forward. To me, this is a very crucial step in each project, which provides me with the understanding of the client towards my work.

work User Needs
  1. Study the business goals and the history of the client’s brand or organisation.
  2. Communicate with the client about the needs of the user.
  3. Communicate with the other team members to understand the technical resources available.
  4. Draft a estimate to determine the checkpoints that align with the goals and fit within the deadlines and technical resources.
poll Preliminary Analysis
  1. Explore the needs of the user.
  2. Research the current competitors in the market.
  3. Gather out points that might help to carry out the project/product.
  4. Sketch out rough ideas.
developer_board UX Design
  1. Split down the ideas into smaller, actionable goals.
  2. Research existing UX patterns for achieving the similar goals/actions.
  3. Understand the edge cases by building a user personas and intent around each user type.
  4. Create user flows to determine how to complete each goal in the best possible way.
  5. Break down the user flows into smaller interconnected components.
  6. Develop wireframes based on the components.
  7. Determine various states for each wireframe e.g. empty, filled, logged in, logged out, error etc.
format_paint Visual Design
  1. Research various design patterns and take inspirations.
  2. Research on frameworks and guidelines if necessary.
  3. Properly experiment with different styles, colors and typography most suited for the project/product.
  4. Transform the wireframes to high fidelity mockups.
  5. Generate design guidelines to help standardize visual properties such as size, style, positioning.
call_split Prototyping
  1. Determine the start, processing and end state for each user flow.
  2. Build interactive prototypes for the client and other team members to demonstrate motion, feedback, state and context.
bug_report Test and finalize
  1. Test the prototype and take user feedback.
  2. Make necessary changes to the prototype and finalise the design.
  3. Finalize the assets for handover to the team and client.
  4. Determine whether the visuals and the interfaces are adaptable at various sizes.
  5. Collaborate with other team members to determine any technical constraints related to the UI.

About myself

Who I am

Hi! I am Durga Venkatesh, a UI UX Designer and a graduate student of Metallurgical & Materials Engineering at IIT Roorkee.

My insatiable curiosity towards art, craft and design developed when I came across action games. Though I could not improve my mediocre art skills, I found interest in the design aesthetics. It all fuelled up after I got into college. Being a part of campus design group, I learned and improved my design skills. My previous work experience helped me understand UI UX design and now I aim to be a Sr. UI / UX designer who specializes in both user interface and user experience.

I’m an avid dreamer who loves action games and would like to change the world. In my spare time, I like to work on side projects, listen songs and play some games like Call of Duty, PUBG and GTA V.
